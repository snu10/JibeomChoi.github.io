About

Greetings! I am a theoretical biologist working at the intersection of evolutionary biology, mathematics, and cancer research. I am currently a Project Research Fellow at the School of Computational Sciences, Korea Institute for Advanced Study (KIAS), supported by a Sejong Science Fellowship.

My research asks how the principles of social evolution — cooperation, conflict, kin selection — play out among cells, and what they reveal about cancer. To study these questions I build mathematical and computational models, drawing on differential equations, evolutionary game theory, and stochastic processes.

Research

Where there is a cooperation, there is a temptation of defection

01

Social evolution of cancer

Tumors as fraternal cooperative organizations — hierarchy, hypertumors, defector dynamics, and the prospect of treating cancer by inducing cells to defect.

02

Kin recognition & greenbeard genes

How recognition error, spatial configuration, and green-beard systems set the stability of cooperation.

03

Evolutionary dynamics of AI agents

Multiple LLM agents can cooperate or defect under AI-as-a-judge framework, to which principles of social evolution can be applied.

04

Mathematical & computational modeling

PDE frameworks and agent-based simulation implemented in MATLAB and Python to capture dynamics of life systems.
